# Eclampsia Project

## Overview
Eclampsia is a condition that can occur during pregnancy, characterized by seizures. This project aims to provide comprehensive resources and tools for understanding, identifying, and managing eclampsia.

## Introduction
Eclampsia is a severe complication of pregnancy where seizures occur in a woman with preeclampsia, a condition characterized by high blood pressure and potential organ failure.

## Symptoms
- Seizures
- Severe headaches
- Blurred vision
- Abdominal pain

## Causes
While the exact cause of eclampsia is unknown, it is believed to be related to the placenta and the blood vessels associated with it.

## Diagnosis
Diagnosis typically involves a review of symptoms, blood pressure measurement, and urine tests.

## Treatment
- Medications to control seizures
- Monitoring and managing blood pressure
- Delivery of the baby, if necessary

## Preventive Measures
- Regular prenatal checkups
- Management of chronic conditions
